Lasagna

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish that has become beloved worldwide for its rich layers of flavor and comforting texture. Made with wide, flat pasta sheets, lasagna is typically layered with a savory filling that combines a hearty meat sauce, creamy ricotta cheese, mozzarella, and a sprinkle of Parmesan for extra depth. The meat sauce, often made with ground beef or sausage, is simmered with tomatoes, garlic, onions, and herbs like basil and oregano, creating a fragrant and flavorful foundation for the dish. Ricotta cheese mixed with eggs and herbs forms a creamy layer that balances the meatiness of the sauce, while mozzarella melts into a gooey, golden topping that adds a satisfying, stretchy texture when served hot.

Each bite of lasagna delivers a blend of textures and tastes, from the chewy pasta to the tender, flavorful filling. Lasagna is often baked in a casserole dish, allowing the layers to meld together as the cheese bubbles and browns on top.
